About


Summary

This database includes over 250 printed sources of Scottish fiddle music, from Bremner's Collection of Scots Reels of 1751 to 1850, omitting sources without basses (because of the bass-led focus of the research project), and publications of songs and airs. The focus is on substantial printed collections, but it also includes a selection of the hundreds of surviving single-sheet publications. Manuscript sources were excluded purely to keep the project to a manageable size, within the time and resources available at this stage.

Finding the limits of the repertoire was a work-in-progress. We began by looking for sources of 'Scottish fiddle music', but the boundaries of this became ever more porous and difficult to define, as Scottish fiddlers were familiar with a more diverse repertoire than our present-day conception of Scottish fiddle music might suggest. Also, the notion of Scottishness in music is difficult to pin down at a time when the notion of a national culture was only gradually taking shape.

Theoretical models for describing and analysing this music are still in the very early stages of development, so a detailed (or definitive) analysis is both inappropriate and probably impossible. The short notes on the music in each source give

  • a summary of the kind of repertoire it contains
  • some features of the basslines
  • particular points of interest that the research team noted on a brief survey of the contents
